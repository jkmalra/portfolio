Explainability becomes useful only when people can understand what happened and what to do next. This framework connects AI compliance, interface design, and decision transparency into a single operating model.

## Context

Most AI governance systems still separate policy from product. Teams maintain standards in one place, model logs in another, and user-facing explanations somewhere else entirely. That split weakens trust and slows response.

## Main body

Explainability only becomes useful when people can act on it. Decision surfaces need to connect policy, model behavior, and user communication in one legible structure.

The framework centers on three layers:

- Evidence: what happened
- Consequence: why it matters
- Intervention: what can be done next

In practice, this means AI products should provide operational narratives rather than hidden confidence scores or compliance theater.

> A trustworthy interface does not merely expose data. It exposes consequence and control.

## Key takeaways

- AI governance must be visible in the interface, not only in policy files.
- Decision context and intervention paths should sit close together.
- Trust improves when users can inspect and challenge automated outcomes.
